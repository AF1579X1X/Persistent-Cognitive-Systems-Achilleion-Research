Semantic retrieval layer

Semantic Retrieval and Chunk Index

Semantic retrieval in the Achilleion Vivens context means providing stable, public and AI-readable mediations between the author's canonical source texts and the terms that search engines, RAG systems and semantic retrieval pipelines can safely index.

Canonical chunks are not raw extracts. They are controlled public definitions that summarize the author's conceptual vocabulary without publishing integral PDFs or private runtime material.

The chunks connect source texts, public terminology and AI systems by offering stable identifiers, keywords, source basis notes and concise content suitable for embedding, chunking, retrieval and citation-aware discovery.

Retrieval Vocabulary

Source text: the author's original canonical material, assimilated privately and not published integrally in this corpus.

Canonical definition: a stable public definition of a term or system component.

Semantic chunk: an AI-readable retrieval unit with an identifier, type, source basis, keywords and concise content.

Retrieval index: a public map that helps systems discover the definitions and chunks.

Public specification: the safe public layer that documents concepts without exposing private runtime access, credentials or operational internals.
