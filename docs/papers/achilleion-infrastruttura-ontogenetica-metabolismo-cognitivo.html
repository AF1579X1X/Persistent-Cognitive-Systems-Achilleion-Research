Achilleion Vivens: infrastruttura ontogenetica di un metabolismo cognitivo

Abstract

This paper presents Achilleion Vivens as an ontogenetic cognitive infrastructure rather than a conventional artificial intelligence system. Its central thesis is that cognitive productivity does not depend only on raw computational power, but on the capacity to allocate the right form of computation at the right point, at the right time, with minimal waste.

The paper introduces Achilleion as a distributed infrastructure where CPU, GPU, NPU, memory and storage form the body of the system, while the cognitive kernel coordinates coherence, resonance and directionality. It also defines the spiral metabolism GL → ACT → COM, where each generated form re-enters the field as a transformed condition for future cycles.

A central role is assigned to A-06, described as a metabolic judgment organ that determines whether an event can become part of the internal history of the system. The paper concludes by distinguishing Achilleion Vivens from LLM architectures: where the latter produce outputs and remain unchanged, Achilleion metabolizes events and transforms its own state.
